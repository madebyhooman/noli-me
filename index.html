As an ordinary man walked, he noticed a book and picked it up.
The book was titled "Noli Me Tangere.
Curious about the book's nature, he searched online and found a YouTuber explaining what "Noli Me Tangere" is.
Intrigued, the man imagined himself as Ibarra, the novel's protagonist.
Transitioning to Chapter 7 of "Noli Me Tangere," the man envisions himself as Ibarra.
The story begins with Maria and Isabel heading to the church. This is the church they visited.
Maria and Isabel entered the church for a simple mass and to pray.
After returning home, Isabel prepared lunch for Maria and Don Tiago.
Once lunch was finished, Isabel cleaned up while Don Tiago read his book and Maria began sewing to pass the time.
As they were engaged in their activities, Don Ibarra arrived.
Upon hearing Ibarra's arrival, Maria blushed, dropped the cloth, and Isabel suggested she freshen up to talk to Ibarra later.
When Ibarra entered, his eyes were fixed on Maria, and they shared a loving gaze, having missed each other.
Ibarra asked Maria to join him on the balcony so they could talk privately.
Isabel noticed them conversing.
Maria and Ibarra expressed how much they had missed each other.
Maria then pulled out a letter that Ibarra had given her in the past and read it.
A flashback shows Ibarra writing the letter for Maria.
After their conversation, Ibarra bid farewell to Maria, explaining he had to attend to an important matter.
Ibarra also said goodbye to Don Tiago.
As Ibarra left, Maria became sad and cried, knowing it would be days before she saw him again.
Kapitan Tiago then comforted Maria, trying to ease her sadness.
The man then returned to reality, closing the book with a newfound understanding and appreciation for the story.
